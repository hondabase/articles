# Pocket Programmer 2

## Overview

The [Pocket Programmer 2](https://www.xtronics.com/memory/EPROM.htm) is a ROM burner designed for programming **MCUs** and various chip types through multiple optional adapters. It was commonly bundled with Hondata ECU tuning packages and remains a popular option for hobbyist tuners.

## Specifications

| Specification | Details |
|---|---|
| **List Price** | $149.95 (as of October 2003) |
| **Supported Platforms** | Windows, DOS |
| **Primary Use** | ROM programming, MCU flashing |
| **Compatibility** | Wide range of chip types with adapter support |

## Capabilities

- Programs **MCUs** and specialized chip variants
- Supports multiple chip families through optional adapters
- Functional Windows and DOS-based software
- Active manufacturer support (Xtronics maintains regular software updates)

## Technical Notes

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Compatibility with specific laptop models has been documented. Thorough testing with your hardware before committing to a ROM burn is recommended.

> [!TIP]
> Consider evaluating alternative ROM programmers (such as the Super Pro Z) if you require maximum compatibility and reliability across diverse systems.

## Known Limitations

- Compatibility issues have been reported with certain laptop platforms (e.g., IBM ThinkPad T20)
- Unit-to-unit variability has been observed in field use
- Performance may be unpredictable with older or unusual hardware configurations

## Conclusion

The Pocket Programmer 2 offers reasonable value for budget-conscious users seeking a polished, supported ROM burning solution. For users prioritizing maximum compatibility and long-term reliability, exploring alternative programmers is advised.
