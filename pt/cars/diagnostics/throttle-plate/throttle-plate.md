# Throttle Plate Operation and Function

The throttle plate is a circular valve located within the throttle body assembly. It acts as the primary mechanical regulator for the volume of air entering the intake manifold.

## Mechanical Function
As the accelerator pedal is depressed, the throttle plate rotates, increasing the cross-sectional area available for airflow. When the pedal is released, the plate returns to a near-closed position, restricting airflow to maintain engine idle speed.

> [!NOTE]
> In most Honda OBD-series applications, the throttle plate is mechanically actuated via a cable connected to the accelerator pedal.

## Diagnostic Considerations
The throttle plate must be properly calibrated to ensure accurate sensor readings and optimal engine performance.

*   **Idle Air Control (IAC):** Because the throttle plate is designed to be nearly closed at idle, the IAC valve is responsible for bypassing a controlled amount of air to maintain a stable idle RPM.
*   **Throttle Position Sensor (TPS):** The TPS is mounted directly to the throttle body shaft. It provides the ECU with a voltage signal corresponding to the exact angle of the throttle plate.

> [!WARNING]
> Do not adjust the factory-set throttle stop screw unless performing a specific service procedure. Altering this setting can lead to incorrect TPS calibration and erratic idle behavior.

## Common Maintenance
*   **Cleaning:** Carbon buildup around the edges of the throttle plate and the bore of the throttle body can cause the plate to stick or result in an unstable idle. Use a dedicated throttle body cleaner and a soft cloth to remove deposits.
*   **Inspection:** Ensure the plate moves smoothly through its full range of motion without binding or catching on the throttle body walls.
