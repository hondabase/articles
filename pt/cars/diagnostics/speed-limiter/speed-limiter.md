# Honda ECU Speed Limiter Operation

A speed limiter restricts the maximum velocity of a vehicle. In most Honda applications, the speed limiter is implemented as a software routine within the ECU that monitors the Vehicle Speed Sensor (VSS) signal. When the vehicle reaches a pre-programmed speed threshold, the ECU initiates a fuel cut to prevent further acceleration.

## Technical Overview

The speed limiter is a standard feature in many factory ECU calibrations, particularly those intended for the Japanese Domestic Market (JDM), which are often factory-restricted to 180 km/h (approx. 112 mph).

> [!NOTE]
> The ECU determines vehicle speed by processing pulses generated by the VSS. If the VSS signal is interrupted or faulty, the ECU may trigger a Check Engine Light (CEL) for code 17 (Vehicle Speed Sensor).

## Bypassing the Speed Limiter

There are two primary methods for addressing speed limiters in Honda ECUs:

1.  **ECU Reprogramming:** Modifying the fuel and ignition maps within the ECU firmware to increase or disable the speed limiter threshold. This is the preferred method for performance tuning.
2.  **Speed Limit Defencer (SLD):** A hardware device installed between the VSS and the ECU. The SLD intercepts the speed signal and modifies the pulse frequency before it reaches the ECU, effectively "tricking" the ECU into reading a lower speed than the vehicle's actual velocity.

> [!WARNING]
> Using a hardware Speed Limit Defencer may cause inaccurate speedometer readings or interfere with other vehicle systems that rely on VSS data, such as automatic transmission shift points or cruise control functionality. Always verify the compatibility of external hardware with your specific ECU variant.
