# Multi Port Fuel Injection (MPFI) Overview

Multi Port Fuel Injection (MPFI) is a fuel delivery system characterized by the use of one dedicated fuel injector per intake runner, with one intake runner per cylinder. 

In a standard four-cylinder Honda engine configuration, this system utilizes four individual injectors to deliver fuel directly into the intake port of each cylinder.

## System Characteristics

*   **Individual Delivery:** Each cylinder receives fuel from its own dedicated injector, allowing for precise control over the air-fuel mixture for every combustion event.
*   **Intake Configuration:** The system requires an intake manifold design where each cylinder has a distinct runner, ensuring that the fuel spray is directed toward the intake valve.
*   **Engine Management:** MPFI systems are controlled by the Engine Control Unit (ECU), which manages injector pulse width and timing based on data from various engine sensors.

> [!NOTE]
> MPFI is distinct from Throttle Body Injection (TBI), which typically uses one or two injectors located in a central throttle body to supply fuel to all cylinders simultaneously.

## Benefits of MPFI
*   **Improved Fuel Atomization:** By placing the injector closer to the intake valve, the fuel is better atomized before entering the combustion chamber.
*   **Increased Efficiency:** Individual cylinder fueling allows the ECU to compensate for minor variations in airflow between cylinders, leading to smoother idle and improved throttle response.
*   **Emissions Control:** Precise fuel delivery enables more effective operation of the catalytic converter and oxygen sensor feedback loops.
