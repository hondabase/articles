# Single Overhead Cam (SOHC) Engine Architecture

Single Overhead Cam (SOHC) engines utilize a single camshaft located in the cylinder head to actuate both intake and exhaust valves. This configuration is the standard architecture for the majority of Honda D-series engines.

## Operational Overview

In an SOHC design, the camshaft is driven by the crankshaft via a timing belt or chain. As the camshaft rotates, the lobes directly actuate the valve train—typically through rocker arms—to manage the intake of air-fuel mixture and the expulsion of exhaust gases.

> [!NOTE]
> Unlike DOHC (Double Overhead Cam) configurations, which utilize separate camshafts for intake and exhaust valves, the SOHC design prioritizes mechanical simplicity and reduced cylinder head mass.

## Key Characteristics

*   **Valve Actuation:** A single camshaft controls both intake and exhaust cycles.
*   **Application:** Primarily found in Honda D-series engines.
*   **Maintenance:** Generally offers easier access for timing belt service and valve clearance adjustments compared to DOHC variants.
