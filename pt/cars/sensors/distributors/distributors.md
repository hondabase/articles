# Honda Ignition Distributor Overview

A distributor is a mechanical and electrical device that routes high-voltage pulses from the ignition coil to the individual spark plugs in the correct firing order. In Honda applications, the distributor housing often integrates critical engine management sensors.

## Functionality
The primary purpose of the distributor is to synchronize the ignition timing with the engine's rotation. By utilizing an internal rotor and cap, the system ensures that the high-voltage spark is delivered to the appropriate cylinder at the precise moment required for combustion.

> [!NOTE]
> In Honda OBD0, OBD1, and OBD2 systems, the distributor assembly typically houses the Crankshaft Position (CKP), Top Dead Center (TDC), and Cylinder Position (CYP) sensors.

## Integrated Sensors
Most Honda distributors contain the following internal sensors, which are essential for the ECU to determine engine speed and position:

*   **CKP (Crankshaft Position Sensor):** Detects crankshaft speed and position.
*   **TDC (Top Dead Center Sensor):** Identifies when the number one piston is at top dead center.
*   **CYP (Cylinder Position Sensor):** Identifies the specific cylinder for sequential fuel injection.

## Maintenance and Diagnostics
Distributor performance is critical for engine efficiency and emissions. Common failure points include:

*   **Internal Coil:** Failure leads to a complete loss of spark.
*   **Ignition Control Module (ICM/Igniter):** Often fails due to heat soak, resulting in intermittent or total spark loss.
*   **Cap and Rotor:** Wear on these components can cause misfires or weak spark delivery.
*   **Shaft Seals:** Internal oil leaks can contaminate the sensors and the ICM, leading to erratic sensor signals or electrical shorts.

> [!WARNING]
> Always verify the firing order and ignition timing according to the specific engine service manual when replacing or servicing distributor components.
