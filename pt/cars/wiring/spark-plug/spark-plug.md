# Spark Plug

Spark plugs receive electrical energy from the [Ignition Coil](/cars/ignition/ignition-coil) and discharge it into the compressed air-fuel mixture in the [Combustion Chamber](/cars/wiring/combustion-chamber).

## Overview

The spark plug is a critical ignition system component responsible for initiating combustion by creating an electrical arc across a gap. This arc ignites the compressed air-fuel mixture at precisely timed intervals, driving the engine's power cycle.

## Function

- Receives high-voltage electrical signal from the ignition coil
- Creates an electrical discharge across the electrode gap
- Ignites the air-fuel mixture in the combustion chamber
- Operates under extreme temperature and pressure conditions

## Key Parameters

| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| Typical voltage | 5,000–40,000 V |
| Gap distance | 0.6–1.4 mm (varies by application) |
| Operating temperature | Up to 900°C |
| Service interval | 10,000–100,000 km (varies) |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Spark plug condition directly affects engine performance, fuel economy, and emissions. Regular inspection and replacement per manufacturer specifications is essential.

> [!TIP]
> Proper gap setting and heat range selection are critical for optimal combustion and engine longevity.
