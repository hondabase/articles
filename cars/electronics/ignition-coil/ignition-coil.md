# Ignition Coil

The ignition coil is a wirewound transformer that steps the car's low battery voltage up to
the tens of thousands of volts needed to jump the spark plug gap and ignite the mixture.

On most older Honda PGM-FI engines the coil lives inside the distributor, alongside the
igniter and the crank and cylinder sensors. The ECU and igniter switch the coil's primary
winding; the collapsing magnetic field induces a high-voltage pulse in the secondary
winding, which the distributor routes to `each` plug in turn. Some swaps and tuning setups
move to an external coil or coil-on-plug.
