# Knock Sensor

[Knock Sensor](/pgmfi/wiki/library/knock-sensor)s can be found on [DOHC](/pgmfi/wiki/library/dohc) [Vtec](/pgmfi/wiki/library/vtec) motors, such as the B16, B18C and H22. Also on [SOHC](/pgmfi/wiki/library/sohc) [Vtec](/pgmfi/wiki/library/vtec) [OBD2](/pgmfi/wiki/library/obd2) motors. A knock sensor is basically a special microphone that "listens" to the sound the engine is making. This is used by a [Knock Board](/pgmfi/wiki/library/knock-board) present in the [ECU](/pgmfi/wiki/library/ecu) to determine if the engine is experiencing [Pre Ignition](/pgmfi/wiki/library/pre-ignition), or [knocking ](/pgmfi/wiki/library/pre-ignition). The sensor / board usually look for the engine's [Detonation Frequency](/pgmfi/wiki/library/detonation-frequency). Conventional wisdom holds that the stock [Knock Board](/pgmfi/wiki/library/knock-board) is all but useless for detecting knock in boosted applications. It appears to be a basic safeguard against bad gas, and nothing more. Conventional wisdom says honda [Knock Board](/pgmfi/wiki/library/knock-board)s freak over forged internals, and detect [knocking ](/pgmfi/wiki/library/pre-ignition) (false-postive) with forged pistons. The stock [Knock Sensor](/pgmfi/wiki/library/knock-sensor) is often used with aftermarket knock detection devices, such as the J+S Safeguard, to detect knock more accurately in boosted motors. See [Knock Sensor Voltages](/pgmfi/wiki/library/knock-sensor-voltages) for information on signals from the knock sensor on a running car.
