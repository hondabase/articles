# ZIF

[ZIF](/cars/electronics/zif) stands for Zero Insertion Force. [ZIF](/cars/electronics/zif) sockets are commonly used in [ECU](/cars/electronics/ecu)s where the [EEPROM](/cars/electronics/eeprom) will be removed and reinserted frequently. "ZIFing an [ECU](/cars/electronics/ecu)" is when you desolder the stock [EEPROM](/cars/electronics/eeprom) and solder a [ZIF](/cars/electronics/zif) socket in its place. Ensure that you choose a [ZIF](/cars/electronics/zif) socket that has the same number of pins as your [EEPROM](/cars/electronics/eeprom). Keep in mind that some [ZIF](/cars/electronics/zif) sockets are physically larger then regular IC sockets. If the IC you want to add a [ZIF](/cars/electronics/zif) socket to has many parts right next to it, it may not fit.
