# `29C256`

the [ATMEL](http://www.atmel.com) AT29C256 chip is the de facto [FLASH](/cars/electronics/flash)-[ROM](/cars/electronics/rom) chip in use. It uses a 5V programming voltage, can be erased repeatedly electronically. They can be reused ***many*** times (about 100,000) without "wearing out." It is not significantly more expensive than [OTP](/cars/electronics/otp) [ROM](/cars/electronics/rom)s, and can save a lot of time compared to [UV-Erase](/cars/electronics/uv-erase) chips when many program changes are needed. This is because [EPROM](/cars/electronics/eprom)s must be erased in a special ultraviolet eraser device which takes 15-30 minutes, which is another advantage of the flash chips - you don't need a UV eraser! These flash chips are pin-compatible with [27 C256](/cars/electronics/27c256) chips. Some people have difficulty with the Atmel chips. Another recommended part is [SST's 27SF256](http://www.sst.com/products.xhtml/parallel_flash/27/SST27SF256) it is also a flash chip (most 27-series chips are UV-Erase only) Sources:

- [http://www.moates.net](http://www.moates.net) (~$5 `each`)
- [http://www.allamerican.com](http://www.allamerican.com) (~$5 `each`, $50 minimum order)
- [http://www.arrow.com](http://www.arrow.com) (~$5 `each`, no minimum order)
- [http://progshop.com/shop/eproms/#29C](http://progshop.com/shop/eproms/#29C) (70PI <$6 `each`)

on page 14 of the [AMD29C256 Datasheet](http://www.atmel.com/dyn/resources/prod_documents/DOC0046.PDF), directly after the base part number, the 2 digits are the speed of the [ROM](/cars/electronics/rom) (90ns; 70, 120 (12) and 150 (15) are also available). Then the first letter: - "J" is PLCC package (square 32-pin package)
- "P" is DIP (this is what you want, unless you're doing your own thing)
- "T" is TSOP

The second letter - operating temp range code: - "C" means commercial and is rated for 0-70C (32F to 158F)
- "I" means industrial and is rated for -40 to 85C (-40F to 185F) If you have a choice, get this one, because it can get very, hot inside your car in the summer.

Note: most [OBD0](/cars/electronics/obd0) PR3 and PW0 [ECU](/cars/electronics/ecu)s I've seen are ***very*** picky about these chips, and frequently throw random solid [CEL](/cars/electronics/cel)s when using them - all speeds, including 90ns. I would advise using other chips in these [ECU](/cars/electronics/ecu)s.
