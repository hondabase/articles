# Desoldering Tools for ECU Modification

Removing factory solder cleanly without damaging delicate copper traces is critical when modifying an ECU. A [Soldering Iron](/cars/ecu/soldering-iron) is used to melt the solder, while a desoldering tool is used to remove it.

## 1. Plunger-Style Desoldering Pumps (Solder Suckers)

These tools use a spring-loaded piston inside a cylinder to generate sudden vacuum suction when a release button is triggered.

* **Operation:** The spring is compressed manually, the Teflon tip is placed directly against the molten solder joint heated by the iron, and the release button is pressed to snap the plunger back, drawing the solder into the chamber.
* **Performance:** Standard plastic models can have inconsistent suction or suffer from recoil that may damage traces if not held steadily. High-quality professional models with silicone tips offer superior performance and stability.

> [!CAUTION]
> Ensure the tool is held firmly against the PCB to prevent recoil from damaging sensitive copper traces.

## 2. Bulb-Style Desoldering Tools

A rubber bulb attached to a hollow tip allows for manual suction generation.

* **Operation:** The bulb is squeezed before heating the joint. The tip is placed on the molten solder, and the bulb is released to draw the solder inside.
* **Performance:** These tools require manual effort and can be tiring during extended use.

> [!WARNING]
> Do not squeeze the bulb while the tip is over the joint. This will blow molten solder back onto the PCB, potentially causing short circuits.

## 3. Solder Wick (Braid)

A fine copper braid coated with rosin flux that draws molten solder out of a joint via capillary action.

* **Operation:** Place the braid directly over the solder joint, press the hot soldering iron tip onto the braid, and allow the solder to be absorbed into the copper weave.
* **Performance:** Excellent for cleaning flat surface-mount pads and clearing stubborn through-holes.

> [!TIP]
> If the braid sticks to the board, apply a small amount of fresh solder or flux to the joint and reheat. Refer to the [Desoldering Tips](/cars/ecu/desoldering-tips) guide for advanced techniques.
