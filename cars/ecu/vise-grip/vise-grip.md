# Locking Pliers (Vise-Grips) for Automotive Hardware

Locking pliers, commonly referred to as Vise-Grips, are a critical component of any automotive toolkit. Their ability to lock onto fasteners with high clamping force makes them indispensable for hardware removal, particularly when dealing with damaged or seized components.

## Functional Overview

The primary utility of locking pliers in a Honda automotive context involves the extraction of fasteners that have rounded heads or stripped threads. By adjusting the tension screw at the base of the handle, the user can calibrate the clamping pressure to ensure a secure grip on various bolt head sizes and shapes.

## Best Practices for Use

> [!WARNING]
> Excessive force can cause the jaws to crush or further deform soft metal fasteners. Always ensure the jaws are seated squarely on the fastener head before applying torque.

> [!CAUTION]
> Avoid using locking pliers on critical high-torque fasteners (e.g., head bolts, suspension pivot bolts) if the fastener head is already compromised. In these instances, use a dedicated bolt extractor socket to prevent further damage.

### Application Scenarios

*   **Stripped Fasteners:** Removing bolts or nuts where standard sockets or wrenches have failed due to rounding.
*   **Stud Extraction:** Providing a secure grip on exposed studs to facilitate removal from engine blocks or exhaust manifolds.
*   **Holding Small Components:** Acting as a temporary clamp for small brackets or electrical connectors during soldering or fabrication tasks.
*   **Broken Bolt Removal:** Grasping the remaining shank of a sheared bolt to rotate it out of a threaded hole.

## Maintenance and Care

To ensure the longevity and effectiveness of the tool:

*   **Lubrication:** Periodically apply a light machine oil to the pivot points and the adjustment screw to maintain smooth operation.
*   **Cleaning:** Remove debris and metal shavings from the serrated teeth of the jaws to ensure maximum grip.
*   **Inspection:** Check the locking mechanism for wear; if the pliers fail to lock securely under pressure, the tool should be replaced to prevent slippage and potential injury.
