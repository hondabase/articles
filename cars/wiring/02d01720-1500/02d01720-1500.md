# VTEC Conversion Guide: "1720" ECU Boards

This guide provides instructions for converting a non-VTEC "1720" board to support VTEC operation.

---

## Required Components

To enable VTEC on a non-VTEC "1720" board, the following components must be installed:

*   **Q26:** [C2785](/cars/sensors/c2785) NPN switching transistor (or a `C144` as a verified alternative).
*   **C60:** 1uF 35V tantalum capacitor (marked "1... 35" with a blue polarity mark).
*   **IC14:** [515X High Side Switch](/cars/rom/515x-high-side-switch).
*   **J10:** Jumper wire.
*   **R67:** 820 Ohm, 1/8W 5% resistor (1k resistor is also acceptable).
*   **RM11:** Voltage divider module (can be substituted with a 10k 8-pin resistor module).
*   **D11:** [Clamping Diode](/cars/rom/clamping-diode).

### Optional Components

The following components are largely considered unnecessary for the conversion:
*   **C75, C89, C72, C74, C88:** 22pF ceramic capacitors (marked "22").
*   **IC13:** [515X High Side Switch](/cars/rom/515x-high-side-switch).

---

## Technical Notes

This board design is one of Honda's early [OBD1](/cars/wiring/obd1) efforts. It is more complex than necessary.

**Regarding IC13:** The `IC13` component is entirely superfluous. It is recommended to remove it and install a jumper wire at `J10` to bypass it. `IC13` and `IC14` are run in series, meaning installing both significantly increases the probability of failure.

---

## Component Locations

*   **C60 & J10:** Top left of the ECU case.
*   **RM11:** Resistor setup.
*   **Q26:** NPN Transistor location.
*   **IC14 & R67:** 5151S driver and resistor.
*   **D11:** Diode located in the upper left of the case.
