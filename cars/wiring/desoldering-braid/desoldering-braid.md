# Desoldering Braid

**Desoldering braid** (also known as solder wick) is a fine-woven, typically copper wire used for removing excess solder from circuit boards.

---

## How it Works
It utilizes "wicking" action, similar to a paper towel absorbing liquid. When heated with a soldering iron, the braid draws molten solder away from the board and into the fabric-like weave of the wire.

## Usage Tips
*   **Effective:** It is an inexpensive and highly effective tool for cleaning up through-hole pads.
*   **Accessibility:** Widely available at most electronics supply stores.

*Always exercise caution when using hot tools on sensitive circuit boards to prevent lifting pads or damaging nearby components.*
