# Throttle Plate Operation and Function

The throttle plate is a butterfly-style valve located within the [Throttle Body](/cars/tuning/throttle-body). It serves as the primary mechanical regulator for airflow entering the intake manifold.

## Functional Overview

As the accelerator pedal is depressed, the throttle plate rotates, increasing the cross-sectional area available for air intake. This mechanical action directly influences the engine's load and RPM by controlling the volume of air permitted to enter the combustion chamber.

> [!NOTE]
> The throttle plate is linked to the Throttle Position Sensor (TPS), which provides the ECU with real-time data regarding the plate's angle. This data is critical for calculating fuel injection duration and ignition timing.

## Maintenance and Inspection

Proper operation of the throttle plate is essential for stable idle and smooth throttle response. 

*   **Carbon Deposits:** Over time, carbon buildup around the edges of the plate and the throttle body bore can cause the plate to stick or result in an erratic idle.
*   **Seal Integrity:** Ensure the throttle shaft seals remain intact to prevent vacuum leaks, which can lead to lean running conditions.
*   **Calibration:** If the throttle body is removed or replaced, the TPS must be recalibrated to ensure the ECU correctly identifies the "closed" and "wide-open" throttle positions.

> [!CAUTION]
> Avoid using harsh solvents on the throttle plate if it features a specialized molybdenum coating, as this can strip the finish and lead to premature wear or sticking.
