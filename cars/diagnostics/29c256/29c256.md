# `29C256`

the [ATMEL](http://web.archive.org/web/20171230124942/http://www.atmel.com/) AT29C256 chip is the de facto [FLASH](/cars/rom/flash)-[ROM](/cars/rom/rom) chip in use. It uses a 5V programming voltage, can be erased repeatedly electronically. They can be reused ***many*** times (about 100,000) without "wearing out." It is not significantly more expensive than [OTP](/cars/rom/otp) [ROM](/cars/rom/rom)s, and can save a lot of time compared to [UV-Erase](/cars/rom/uv-erase) chips when many program changes are needed. This is because [EPROM](/cars/rom/eprom)s must be erased in a special ultraviolet eraser device which takes 15-30 minutes, which is another advantage of the flash chips - you don't need a UV eraser! These flash chips are pin-compatible with [27 C256](/cars/ecu/27c256) chips. Some people have difficulty with the Atmel chips. Another recommended part is [SST's 27SF256](http://web.archive.org/web/20090725071450/http://www.sst.com:80/products.xhtml/parallel_flash/27/SST27SF256) it is also a flash chip (most 27-series chips are UV-Erase only) Sources:

on page 14 of the [AMD29C256 Datasheet](http://web.archive.org/web/20171230124942/http://www.atmel.com//dyn/resources/prod_documents/DOC0046.PDF), directly after the base part number, the 2 digits are the speed of the [ROM](/cars/rom/rom) (90ns; 70, 120 (12) and 150 (15) are also available). Then the first letter: - "J" is PLCC package (square 32-pin package)
- "P" is DIP (this is what you want, unless you're doing your own thing)
- "T" is TSOP

The second letter - operating temp range code: - "C" means commercial and is rated for 0-70C (32F to 158F)
- "I" means industrial and is rated for -40 to 85C (-40F to 185F) If you have a choice, get this one, because it can get very, hot inside your car in the summer.

**Note:** most [OBD0](/cars/rom/obd0) PR3 and PW0 [ECU](/cars/ecu/ecu)s I've seen are ***very*** picky about these chips, and frequently throw random solid [CEL](/cars/wiring/cel)s when using them - all speeds, including 90ns. I would advise using other chips in these [ECU](/cars/ecu/ecu)s.
