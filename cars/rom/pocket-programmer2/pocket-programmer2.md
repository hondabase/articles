# Pocket Programmer2

The [Pocket Programmer 2](https://www.xtronics.com/memory/EPROM.htm) lists at $149.95 as of October 9, 2003. It has not fluctuated in price much. It supports a wide variety of chips, has decent software for windows and dos. It can burn [MCU](/cars/rom/mcu)s and all sorts of weird chips through a variety of add-on adapters. This is a very common [Rom Burner](/cars/ecu/rom-burner) as it was packaged with [Hondata](http://www.hondata.com). IMHO, the pocket programmer 2 isn't a bad deal, particularly for someone seeking a polished product. Xtronics technical support is pretty decent, they keep the software up to date but I've seen many of these programmers be very, very, very peculiar. I never successfully burned a [ROM](/cars/rom/rom) with my old thinkpad T20, but it worked fine with my current thinkpad T21. Not a bad burner for the price, but after using the [Super Pro Z](/cars/diagnostics/super-pro-z) I'd never buy one again. -Dave
