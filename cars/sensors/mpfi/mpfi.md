# Multi Port Fuel Injection (MPFI) Overview

Multi Port Fuel Injection (MPFI) is a fuel delivery strategy where each intake runner is equipped with an individual fuel injector. This configuration ensures that each cylinder receives fuel through its own dedicated intake path.

## System Characteristics

In an MPFI system, the number of injectors corresponds directly to the number of cylinders in the engine. For example, a standard four-cylinder Honda engine utilizes four injectors, each positioned to spray fuel directly into the intake port of its respective cylinder.

> [!NOTE]
> MPFI is distinct from Throttle Body Injection (TBI), which typically utilizes one or two injectors located in a central throttle body assembly to feed all cylinders simultaneously.

## Advantages of MPFI

*   **Improved Fuel Atomization:** By placing the injector closer to the intake valve, fuel has less distance to travel, reducing wall wetting and improving mixture homogeneity.
*   **Precise Fuel Metering:** The Engine Control Unit (ECU) can manage fuel delivery for each cylinder more accurately, allowing for better air-fuel ratio control across varying engine loads and RPMs.
*   **Enhanced Throttle Response:** The reduction in fuel travel time from the injector to the combustion chamber results in more immediate engine response to throttle inputs.
