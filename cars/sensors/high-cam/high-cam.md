# VTEC High Cam Profile

In a [VTEC](/cars/sensors/vtec) engine, the High Cam profile is engaged at higher [RPM](/cars/sensors/rpm) ranges. This camshaft profile is specifically engineered to maximize volumetric efficiency and power output at high engine speeds.

## Operational Overview

The VTEC system utilizes a dual-profile camshaft design. While the [Low Cam](/cars/sensors/low-cam) is optimized for fuel economy and low-end torque, the High Cam features increased lift and duration to allow for greater airflow into the combustion chamber.

> [!NOTE]
> The transition between the Low Cam and High Cam profiles is controlled by the ECU based on specific load, speed, and temperature parameters.

## Camshaft Comparison

| Feature | Low Cam | High Cam |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Goal** | Fuel Economy / Torque | Peak Power |
| **Lift** | Lower | Higher |
| **Duration** | Shorter | Longer |
