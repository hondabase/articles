# P75 ECU Technical Reference

The P75 ECU is the factory engine control unit for the 1996–2000 Acura Integra LS/GS (B18B1 engine). While primarily an OBD2-based unit, it shares significant architecture with the OBD1 P74 ECU.

## Overview
The P75 is designed for non-VTEC B-series engines. It manages fuel and ignition mapping optimized for the B18B1 platform. 

> [!NOTE]
> For information regarding the OBD1 variant of this ECU, refer to the [P74 documentation](/cars/sensors/p74).

## Hardware Variations
The P75 underwent several revisions throughout its production cycle to accommodate changes in OBD2 emissions standards and internal board layouts.

| Revision | OBD Version | Chassis Years | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| P75-A01 | OBD2a | 1996–1998 | Standard LS/GS mapping |
| P75-A02 | OBD2b | 1999–2000 | Updated processor and emissions logic |

## Compatibility
The P75 is frequently used in engine swaps due to its robust, non-VTEC management. 

> [!IMPORTANT]
> When converting between OBD1 and OBD2, ensure the use of a proper conversion harness. The P75 requires specific sensor inputs (such as the CKF sensor) when running in an OBD2 environment that are not present on OBD1 chassis.
