# VTEC Low Cam Profile

In a VTEC-equipped engine, the Low Cam profile is engaged during low-RPM operation. This camshaft profile is specifically engineered to prioritize fuel economy, emissions compliance, and a smooth, stable idle.

> [!NOTE]
> The engine management system transitions from the Low Cam to the High Cam profile once specific load and RPM thresholds are met, allowing for increased airflow and power at higher engine speeds.

## Camshaft Characteristics

The Low Cam profile features smaller lobe lift and shorter duration compared to the High Cam profile. This design optimizes intake velocity and volumetric efficiency at lower engine speeds.

*   **Primary Function:** Fuel efficiency and idle quality.
*   **Operational Range:** Idle up to the VTEC engagement point.
*   **Related Component:** [High Cam](/cars/sensors/high-cam)
